Sound Familiar?

The system has a blind spot. You're standing in it.

There's a whole group of people who earn too much for government help — and get quoted the worst prices because of it. If any of these four sound like you, you're exactly who I help.

01

You're self-employed and over the subsidy cliff

Consultants, business owners, freelancers, 1099s — your income disqualifies you from ACA tax credits, so you're paying full sticker price for a marketplace plan built for someone else. If you're paying full price anyway, why not shop the entire market?

Paying full price for less
02

Your employer plan costs too much for what it is

High premiums, a deductible you'll never hit before the year resets, and networks that keep shrinking. Here's what HR never mentions: you're allowed to shop privately instead of accepting whatever landed in your enrollment packet.

HR's best isn't the best
03

Adding your family feels like a second mortgage

Your own coverage through work might be fine — until you add a spouse and kids and the price triples. Often the smartest move is keeping yourself on the work plan and insuring your family privately for a fraction of the cost.

Dependents priced like luxuries
04

You just opened your COBRA letter

Laid off, changed jobs, retired early, or aged off a parent's plan — and now COBRA wants 2–3× what a private plan often costs for similar or better coverage. You have a 60-day election window. Use it to compare before you commit.
